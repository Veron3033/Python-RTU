Once there was a kind girl named Cinderella.
She lived with a proud stepmother.
Two stepsisters laughed at her old dress.
Each day she cleaned the house.
She swept ashes from the cold hearth.
She sang softly while she worked.
News came of a royal ball.
Every young woman wished to go.
The stepsisters chose ribbons and shoes.
Cinderella was left at home in tears.
Then her fairy godmother appeared.
With a wand she changed the night.
A pumpkin became a shining coach.
Mice became horses with quick feet.
Rags became a silver gown.
Glass slippers sparkled on her feet.
The godmother warned her about midnight.
At the palace the prince saw her.
They danced as if time were sleeping.
When the clock began to strike, she ran.
One glass slipper fell on the stair.
The prince searched the kingdom with that shoe.
The slipper fit only Cinderella.
She forgave her family and married the prince.
